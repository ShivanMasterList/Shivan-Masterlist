Behaviors

Shivans spend most of their day looking for food and patroling their territory. They are very territorial and ageressive toward strangers, mostly towards fellow Shivans.

Shivans are more active during the days, like all reptiles they really enjoy sunbathing, and become more reclusive at night. A safe hiding place is important if you want to attempt to train one.

Shivans are solitary animals and fearful of humans but once tamed the are very loyal and helpful. They are not afraid to sacrifice their lifes for their owner.

Respect is earned. Shivans will be obedient only if their owner is respectful and faithful to their Shivan. Perfect relationships between humans and Shivan are extremly difficult to achieve.
